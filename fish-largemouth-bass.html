The Species

About Largemouth Bass

Largemouth bass are the undisputed king of South Bay freshwater fishing. California produces some of the largest largemouth in the world ‚Äî 21 of the biggest 25 largemouth bass in the US were caught in California ‚Äî and while our local reservoirs aren't Clear Lake or Castaic, they hold fish that will absolutely surprise you. Seven-pound bass are caught at Calero every spring. Double-digit fish are not unheard of.

Largemouth thrive in the warm, structured reservoirs of Santa Clara County. They are ambush predators, lurking near weed edges, submerged timber, rocky points, and dock pilings. Understanding their seasonal patterns ‚Äî spawning in spring, retreating deep in summer, feeding aggressively in fall ‚Äî is the key to consistent success.

California Regulations

Rules to Know

  • Minimum size: 12 inches (statewide for all black bass)
  • Daily bag limit: 5 fish combined (largemouth, smallmouth, and spotted bass)
  • No seasonal closure ‚Äî bass fishing is open year-round in California
  • Live bait prohibited at all Santa Clara County reservoirs ‚Äî artificial or dead/prepared bait only
  • All Santa Clara County reservoirs are catch-and-release only ‚Äî no bass may be kept
  • A valid California Sport Fishing License is required for anglers 16 and older

Always verify current rules at CDFW.ca.gov before fishing.
